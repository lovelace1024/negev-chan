How to Say “Black” in Hebrew – Ulpan La-Inyan

שָׁחוֹר

If you know an Israeli whose last name is שחור
, they were probably Schwartz (German and Yiddish for black) a few generations back.
Here’s an example of שחור
, the grammatically-masculine version of black:
הרכב שלי שחור.
My car (vehicle) is black.
If what we’re describing is grammatically feminine, we’ll use שחורה
, for example:
המכונית השחורה שם היא שלי.
The black car over there is mine.
Yes, both רכב
 and מכונית
 mean car – one is grammatically masculine, while the other is feminine.
